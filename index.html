Accessibility for Software Engineering

1st Workshop on Accessibility in Software Engineering

Co-located with ICSE 2026, Rio de Janeiro

About the workshop

The 1st Workshop on Accessibility in Software Engineering aims to bring together researchers and practitioners to discuss the challenges and opportunities in integrating accessibility into software engineering practices. The workshop will provide a platform for sharing experiences, discussing research findings, and exploring new ideas in the field of accessibility in software development.

Accessibility is a critical aspect of software engineering, ensuring that software products are usable by people with disabilities. Despite the growing awareness of accessibility issues, there is still a significant gap in the integration of accessibility practices in software development processes. This workshop aims to address this gap by fostering collaboration between researchers, practitioners, and educators in the field.

The workshop will feature keynote speakers, paper presentations, and panel discussions. The workshop will provide a collaborative environment to share and discuss the latest research, and explore new ideas in accessibility for software engineering.

Workshop Goals

  • To raise awareness of the importance of accessibility in software engineering
  • To share recent research in accessibility
  • To foster collaboration between researchers, practitioners, and educators
  • To explore new ideas and solutions for improving accessibility in software engineering

Target Audience

The workshop is open to anyone interested in accessibility in software engineering, including researchers, practitioners, educators, and students. We particularly encourage participation from people with disabilities to share their experiences and perspectives on accessibility in software engineering.

Types of Submissions

We welcome the following types of submissions:

  • Research papers addressing a topic related to accessibility and software engineering (up to 8 pages plus 2 pages references)
  • Experience reports providing anecdotal evidence about an experience related to accessibility in software engineering and interpreting the experience with actionable insights, advice, and lessons learned. This experience can be in an industry or academic setting (up to 8 pages plus 2 pages references)
  • Education papers relating to teaching accessibility in either an academic or an industry setting (up to 8 pages plus 2 pages references)
  • Position papers providing new ideas, emerging results, or interesting directions for an accessibility related topic (up to 4 pages plus 1 pages references)
  • Tool papers describing a tool designed to improve accessibility (up to 5 pages plus 1 page references)
  • Demonstrations of tools enable a practical 15-minute demonstration of an accessibility related tool including those still under development. Please provide a short abstract explaining the tool. (2 page abstract including references).
  • Tutorialso teach the community about an accessibility related topic in a session up to 30 minutes. Please provide a short abstract describing the topic, goal, and expected outcomes of the tutorial
  • Extended abstracts includes shorter research, experience, education, position, and tool papers (up to 5 pages including references). Extended abstracts are free of APC charges

Call for Papers

We seek papers covering a broad range of accessibility-related topics and encourage submissions from academics and industry practitioners on any aspects of accessibility within software engineering, including but not limited to:

  • AI-based tools to detect and fix accessibility problems in software engineering
  • AI-based tools for translating regulations into practical coding tactics and code designs
  • Instructional methods and tools that teach software engineering students about accessibility
  • Open-Source tools for accessibility
  • Accessibility pragmatics for working with particular Operating Systems, libraries, and tools.
  • Mixed ability collaboration in software engineering
  • Learn how to make (custom) software tools accessible
  • Engineering cross-platform issues in accessibility
  • Versioning issues with accessibility
  • Accessible software in specific domains (CAD, digital effects, etc)
  • Social model of disability in software tool design
  • Engineering design/architecture issues' impact on accessibility (e.g., accessible starvation)
  • Intersectionality in disabilities (e.g., deaf-blind, chronic illness)
  • Supporting cognitive decline in software development
  • Career trajectory and outcomes for software engineers with disabilities
  • Policy work to support developers with disabilities (e.g., being on the WCAG board)

Submission Guidelines

  • Submissions should be formatted according to the ACM Conference Proceedings template.
  • Papers should be submitted via the workshop's HotCRP submission system.
  • Submissions must follow the latest IEEE Submission and Peer Review Policy and ACM Policy on Authorship
  • All submissions will be peer-reviewed by at least two members of the program commitee. They will be evaluated on: (i) relevance to the workshop, and potential to stimulate interesting discussions, (ii) novelty, and (iii) presentation quality.
